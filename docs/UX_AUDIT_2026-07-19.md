# MS1/MS2 Workspace UX Audit

## Audit Scope

Combined UX and screenshot-level accessibility review of three owner-provided images:
the current MS1/MS2 screen, the launch-time terminal windows, and the proposed product
layout. Goal: determine whether the tested UI represents the final product and capture
the corrective direction before MS3.

## Verdict

The tested screen is a useful engineering harness but is not acceptable as the final
SimForge workspace. The proposed three-column layout is structurally aligned with the
confirmed product requirements and is now the workspace contract in `UX_WORKSPACE.md`.

## Step Findings

1. **Current foundation screen - poor product health.** It exposes milestone and
   fixture language, mixes credential setup with authoring, lacks conversations and an
   embedded viewport, and presents a sample goal as if the user entered it. The stale
   `Blender waiting` state weakens trust. Strengths are visible mode separation,
   restrained visual styling, and an auditable activity list.
2. **One-click launch - failing.** Two terminal surfaces obscure the app, contradict
   the no-terminal requirement, and make it unclear which window may be safely closed.
   The activity record showed authenticated Blender sessions dropping exactly 30
   seconds after connection. This was a real launcher/bridge defect, not visual polish.
3. **Owner layout - strong direction with unresolved visual details.** The left history
   rail, central authoring surface, bottom mode-aware composer, and right
   viewport/activity/export dock create a coherent desktop hierarchy. Provider secrets
   should move to Settings while active routing remains visible. Panel resizing,
   narrow-window behavior, exact typography/color, and detailed interaction states
   still require implementation and visual QA.

## Accessibility Risks

Screenshots alone cannot verify keyboard operation, focus visibility, screen-reader
labels, zoom reflow, or contrast ratios. The current small uppercase labels and dense
activity metadata may be difficult at high zoom. Connection, revision freshness,
validation severity, and model routing need text/icon labels rather than color-only
signals. Resizable panels require keyboard-accessible alternatives and preserved focus.

## Required Corrections

- Use a windowless shortcut and Blender launcher; keep the authenticated bridge alive
  after handshake and automatically reconnect after app restart.
- Poll or subscribe to bridge state so the header cannot remain visually stale.
- Label the interim screen **Engineering preview** and turn the sample goal into a
  placeholder.
- Use `UX_WORKSPACE.md` for MS3/MS5 panel placement and the full MS6 workspace.

## Evidence Limits

The audit is based on static owner screenshots plus local inspection of the current
desktop build. It does not claim WCAG compliance or approve a final visual system. A
same-viewport reference-versus-build comparison remains mandatory when MS6 is built.
